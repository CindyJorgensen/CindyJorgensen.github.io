Food drive illustrates Scouts' impact

By Necia P. Seamons, Citizen editor
The Preston Citizen

The food drive held Saturday illustrated the potential impact on the community that will come about with the end of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints’ sponsorship of the Boy Scouts of America program.

The current Franklin District in the Trapper Trails Scouting Council is expected to shrink from 15,000 members to 5,000, said Bob Wright, a member of the district’s council.

The Trapper Trails Council currently has 15 districts, and will reorganize the Cache Valley area and Southestern Idaho area it covers into five districts, said Wright.

The Scout’s food drive provides food for 6-8 months for the Preston Community Food Pantry, said Wright. It is the center’s largest provider of donated foodstuffs, he said.

The US Postal Service holds an annual drive to help as well.
